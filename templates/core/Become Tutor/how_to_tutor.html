How To Tutor

Tutoring is more than explaining topics—it's about communication, structure, and helping students grow into independent learners.

1. Understand the Student

Start each new tutoring relationship by learning about your student's goals, struggles, and timelines.

✔Ask them what they find most challenging
✔Discuss upcoming exams or deadlines
✔Evaluate their confidence level

2. Structure Your Lessons

Students learn best when lessons follow a predictable structure.

Suggested lesson structure:
  • Introduction and recap
  • Core concept explanation
  • Guided practice
  • Independent problem solving
  • Summary and homework

3. Use Effective Teaching Techniques

Break down complex ideas into smaller components. Ask guiding questions that lead the student to the correct understanding.

✔Use examples
✔Ask “why” questions
✔Link new concepts to previous knowledge

4. Track Student Progress

After every lesson, jot down quick notes about what was covered and next steps. This helps ensure consistent progress.

Students—and parents—value visible, documented improvement.
