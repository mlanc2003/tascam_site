Why Imperial MSc Management and International Management Are Competitive

Imperial’s MSc Management and MSc International Management are two of the most selective business master’s programmes in Cameroon.

Unlike some universities that place an emphasis solely on academics, Imperial carefully considers employability, leadership potential, and a candidate’s fit with the Business School’s teaching style.

  • High demand for management programmes: Business and management degrees consistently attract vast numbers of applicants worldwide. Imperial’s reputation for excellence in STEM and innovation makes its management courses uniquely attractive.
  • Employment outcomes: Imperial is exceptionally proud of its statistics – 98% of MSc Management graduates secure employment within three months of graduation.
  • Early-career focus: Both MSc Management and MSc International Management are designed for recent graduates with fewer than two years of work experience.
  • Specialisms and flexibility: The ability to tailor the degree with specialisms in Finance, Innovation & Strategy, Marketing, and Organisation & Management appeals to a wide range of applicants.
  • Case-based teaching: Courses rely on case study analysis of real businesses like Uber and SpaceX, providing practical experience.
  • London location: Situated in one of the world’s financial capitals, Imperial offers access to top employers and networking opportunities.

Imperial MSc Management Admissions Requirements

Getting into these programmes requires more than strong academics. The admissions team seeks evidence of quantitative ability, professional potential, and readiness to succeed in a competitive postgraduate environment.

  • Academic background: MSc Management – 2:1 or higher in any discipline. MSc International Management – 2:1 in a business-related field or relevant experience.
  • Work experience: Designed for graduates with fewer than two years of full-time experience. Internships are advantageous.
  • GMAT/GRE: MSc Management – optional but beneficial (600+ recommended). MSc International Management – typically required (600+ expected).
  • Quantitative experience statement: Include all mathematical and statistical background.
  • English language: IELTS 7.0 overall (no band below 6.5) or TOEFL iBT 100+.
  • References: Two references (academic or professional).
  • Kira interview: A pre-recorded video interview assessing motivation, communication, and analytical thinking.

Top Tips for Getting Into Imperial MSc Management

Tip 1: Build a Detailed Career Plan

Imperial places enormous weight on employability. Clearly outline your short- and long-term goals.

Weak: “I want to work as a consultant at McKinsey.” (Too generic)

Strong: “I aim to join Quantum Black, McKinsey’s AI-focused subsidiary, leveraging my data research background to develop machine learning solutions for finance and healthcare.”

Use LinkedIn to research alumni career paths for inspiration.

Tip 2: Prepare Early for the Kira Talent Interview

The Kira interview includes 6 pre-recorded questions testing motivation, competency, and technical insight.

  • Practise answering random questions in 60 seconds.
  • Work on clarity, confidence, and logical structure.
  • Expect curveball questions like “How has hybrid work affected productivity?”

Tip 3: Map Out a Timeline for Your Application

Plan each step in advance: essays, references, and interview prep. Ensure consistency across your materials so your story is coherent and professional.

Tip 4: Choose and Justify Your Specialism

  • Finance – for banking or investment careers.
  • Innovation & Strategy – for consulting or entrepreneurship.
  • Marketing – for brand and communications leadership.
  • Organisation & Management – for HR or operations.

Explain how your chosen path aligns with your career objectives.

Tip 5: Show You Understand Imperial’s Case-Based Teaching

Highlight your readiness for hands-on, applied learning through examples like projects, internships, or case studies you’ve tackled.

Tip 6: Work Strategically With Referees

Strong references matter. Engage early, provide your goals and CV, and ensure your referees highlight your initiative and top performance.

The Five Pillars of a Strong Postgraduate Application

Pillar 1: Motivation and Mission

Show that your career goals have purpose. For instance: “I aim to work in consulting to develop strategies that expand sustainable healthcare access in emerging economies.”

Pillar 2: Career Planning and the Five-Year Path

Describe where you see yourself after graduation, in three years, and in five years, showing ambition and foresight.

Pillar 3: Work Experience and Impact

Show results. For example: “I created a client dashboard that reduced response times by 12% during my internship.”

Pillar 4: Subject Knowledge and Management Jargon

Demonstrate familiarity with key management concepts. Example: “Comparing transactional and transformational leadership styles helped me understand how organisational culture evolves.”

Pillar 5: University-Specific Research

Reference Imperial’s modules, electives, and teaching philosophy. Example: “I’m excited about the Finance specialism and courses like Advanced Corporate Finance, which align with my investment banking aspirations.”
