Ethics & Academic Integrity Training for Tutors

Updated January 2026 · TASCAM Training Team

Ethical tutoring lies at the heart of TASCAM Co Ltd’s mission. We prioritise academic integrity, professional conduct, and responsible support that empowers students without crossing ethical boundaries.

This guide provides clear rules, scenarios, and best-practice expectations for all tutors.

1. What Counts as Ethical Tutoring?

Ethical tutoring means supporting student understanding—not completing their work for them. Tutors must:

  • Promote independent learning.
  • Explain concepts, not provide finished answers.
  • Respect academic integrity policies for all institutions.
  • Guide students through the thinking process.

2. Strict Prohibited Actions

  • Completing assignments, essays, or dissertations for students.
  • Providing model answers that can be submitted.
  • Editing or rewriting full assignments beyond feedback.
  • Participating in exams, quizzes, or online assessments.

3. Acceptable Support Methods

  • Providing feedback on structure, clarity, or argument strength.
  • Explaining theories, concepts, and methodologies.
  • Walking students through worked examples.
  • Helping students plan research and approach questions.

All tutors must acknowledge and comply with TASCAM’s Academic Integrity Policy before beginning work with students.
