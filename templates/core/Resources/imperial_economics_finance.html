Entry Requirements for Imperial EFDS

Securing a place on Imperial’s EFDS programme starts with meeting the formal entry requirements. These are already set at a very high level, and in reality, most successful applicants will go far beyond the minimum.

A-Level Requirements:

  • Minimum offer: A*AA
  • Subjects: A* in Mathematics (non-negotiable). AA in two other subjects. Further Mathematics or Economics are useful, but not strictly required.
  • General Studies or Critical Thinking are not accepted.

International Baccalaureate (IB) Requirements:

  • Minimum offer: 39 points overall.
  • Higher Level: 7 in Mathematics, 6 in a second subject, and 6 in a third subject.

Admissions Test: TMUA

All applicants must sit the Test of Mathematics for University Admission (TMUA). This is a two-paper exam assessing mathematical reasoning and problem-solving. The test is time-pressured and demanding, so preparation well in advance is essential.

English Language Requirements:

For non-native speakers of English, Imperial requires proof of proficiency through recognised qualifications such as IELTS or TOEFL. Most competitive programmes, including EFDS, will expect the higher level (IELTS 7.0 overall, with no band lower than 6.5).

Why This Course Is Unique

EFDS is not just a standard economics course with a data add-on, nor a traditional finance programme with some coding modules thrown in. Instead, it has been designed from the ground up to combine three high-demand disciplines into a single, future-focused degree.

A Course Built for the Future

Imperial has deliberately structured EFDS to reflect the evolving demands of global employers.

  • Economics: Build strong foundations in micro, macro, and applied economics.
  • Finance: Gain fluency in financial modelling, asset pricing, and risk management.
  • Data Science: Learn to code, apply statistical models, and harness machine learning techniques to solve real-world problems.

The Imperial Advantage

  • A STEM-based learning environment that sets EFDS apart from traditional economics faculties.
  • Close ties to London’s financial and tech sectors for internship and networking opportunities.
  • A teaching philosophy blending theory with practice, from coding projects to financial case studies.

Top Tips for Getting Into Imperial EFDS

Tip 1: Excel at Mathematics

Mathematics is at the heart of EFDS. Imperial applicants must sit a 30-minute maths quiz based on the AQA A-level syllabus. Past candidates describe it as accessible but fast-paced, testing fluency under pressure.

  • Drill past A-level questions until you can solve them quickly and accurately.
  • Focus on calculus, algebra, probability, and statistics.
  • Practise under timed conditions to simulate test pressure.

Beyond tests, showcase quantitative strength in your personal statement and predicted grades. Highlight maths challenges or coding projects that show you’re confident with numbers.

Tip 2: Create a Unique Identity in Your Personal Statement

The challenge is combining economics, finance, and data science into a single narrative. Build your identity around quantitative methods and show how mathematics links to real-world problems.

‘During my internship at a fintech startup, I built a Python script to clean and analyse large financial datasets. This experience not only improved my coding skills but also showed me how statistical modelling could be applied to detect market anomalies, a link between data science and finance that I now want to explore academically.’

This example is strong because it’s specific, interdisciplinary, and reflective.

FAQ About How to get into Imperial for Economics, Finance & Data Science

What is the hardest course to get into at Imperial?

EFDS is expected to be Imperial’s hardest course, with fewer than 3 offers per 100 applicants. It combines three in-demand subjects: economics, finance, and data science, preparing students for quantitative finance, fintech, and policy roles.

Is Imperial better than LSE?

LSE excels in social sciences, especially economics. Imperial, however, leads in STEM, mathematics, and data science. For students seeking careers at the intersection of tech and finance—like algorithmic trading or data-driven consulting—Imperial’s EFDS offers a uniquely modern edge.

How hard is it to get into Imperial University?

Imperial’s overall acceptance rate is around 10–15%, but EFDS is expected under 3%. Applicants need exceptional maths ability, originality, and interview skills to stand out.

What kind of careers can EFDS graduates go into?

EFDS graduates are highly sought after in quantitative finance, consulting, fintech, and policymaking. Imperial’s London location provides access to internships and networking within the City and Canary Wharf.
