What is the Imperial acceptance rate?

In 2021, the Imperial acceptance rate was around 11.5% (based on data by UCAS). The university received 28,700 applications and offered places to 3,305 students. It’s worth noting that Imperial and LSE have particularly low acceptance rates because they offer a lesser variety of courses and thus have fewer spaces for applicants.

Imperial’s acceptance rates for its most competitive courses are also significantly lower than its average acceptance rate. For example, the admissions rate for Computing (Games, Vision and Interaction) is just 5.2%. Does Imperial give contextual offers?

Imperial doesn’t offer systematic contextual offers for its courses (unlike UCL and LSE), but it guarantees you an interview and/or offer if you come from a disadvantaged background and your predicted grades meet the minimum college entry standard at A-level or an equivalent level qualification in the relevant subjects. If you’re eligible for a guaranteed interview and/or offer, ensure that you pursue this.

Insider tip: If your school has limited resources, it’s good to apply for Oxbridge as well as Imperial to gain more support from your school. Apply earlier and do whatever Oxbridge requires in addition to Imperial’s process to unlock that support.

Insider information on Imperial

Your referee

A good referee is important. Ideally, choose someone who teaches within your discipline and knows you well. Keep them informed about your extracurriculars, readings, and achievements so they can advocate effectively for you as a student and as a person.

Timing: postgraduates

Create your application portal early—Imperial has additional sections (career prep, quantitative/software skills) that take time. Postgraduate applications are rolling; aim to submit when applications open, not near the deadline.

Bonus tip from Dr Leo (Imperial alumnus): Network with professors and trusted contacts; flagged applications and strong recommendations can differentiate you among similar CVs. “Humble, hungry and well connected” goes far.

Don’t be deterred by a lack of access—eligible applicants from low-opportunity backgrounds who meet minimum requirements are guaranteed an interview.

Stand out

Postgraduate programmes that don’t require GMAT/GRE may still value them. Undergraduates can strengthen their profile via tests (TMUA, STEP, MAT) and competitions (Cameroon Maths Challenge). Consider Imperial’s Summer Schools and the Year 12 Sutton Trust Summer School.

Extracurriculars

  • Research-oriented activities and evidence of investigation (archives, assistant roles) are valuable.
  • Clubs like Robotics or Science demonstrate curiosity and investigation.
  • Frame achievements around innovation, leadership, teamwork, and collaboration (“together we are imperial”).
  • Connect travel/volunteering to course-relevant skills; reference museums/exhibitions and documentaries if pertinent.

7 tips for getting into Imperial College London

1. Do you actually know your specific requirements?

Imperial notes most entrants have 7–9 (A–A*) at GCSE (or international equivalent) with at least a 7 (A) in Maths and Sciences. Many courses require AAA–A*A*A; aim to meet or exceed this and secure A*–A in your target subject.

Insider tip: Unlike Oxbridge’s preference for three stellar A levels, Imperial may accept one grade lower overall if you’re taking four A levels (e.g., A*A*A or A*AAA). Always confirm on the department page.

Postgraduates: Most courses require a 2:1 or First.

2. You’ve mentioned a solid 5-year plan, right?

Imperial is career-focused with case-based, practical teaching. Outline a concrete career plan and how the degree—and Imperial’s approach—serve it. For MScs, be explicit about objectives and (where relevant) a research proposal’s uniqueness.

3. Lay the ‘groundwork’ for acceptance

Seek relevant work experience or subject-aligned extracurriculars (e.g., coding projects for Computing). Undergraduates can explore Imperial’s Year 12 Work Experience Programme. Leadership roles, sector events, and networking can all help.

4. Invest in your personal statement

Make your statement course-centric, evidence passion via experiences and reading, and show work ethic, time management, and communication. Engineering applicants should tailor to their specific branch; Medicine applicants should highlight maturity, stress management, teamwork, and leadership.

Note: UCAS will change personal statements to short questions from January 2025 (October 2024 for Oxbridge). Until then, write the traditional statement.

5. How to ACTUALLY go beyond your school syllabus

Go deep into niche subtopics with academic sources; reference Imperial research and free online courses. Tie subject knowledge to current affairs and department interests to show intellectual initiative.

6. Don’t trip on your admissions test

Admissions tests matter as much as grades. Imperial commonly uses UCAT (Medicine), ESAT (Mech Eng/Physics), and MAT (Maths). Some offers include MAT and/or STEP conditions. Postgraduates may need GMAT/GRE (650+ as a minimum guide).

Start early, practise extensively (logic included), and aim above typical offer-holder benchmarks.

7. Remember the purpose of your interview

Many departments interview, but formats vary widely (recruitment days with group tasks/tests, Kira pre-recorded interviews, panel/Q&A splits). Medicine uses MMIs; Civil may assess teamwork in group settings. Prepare for your course’s format.

  • Be ready to discuss your personal statement, readings, and experiences.
  • Use concrete examples; think aloud on technical questions.
  • Practise online platforms (e.g., Kira Talent) and group dynamics.
  • Show collaboration, curiosity, and independent thinking.

Please note: Imperial can respond as late as March.

FAQs

What subjects are Imperial known for? What subjects should I take?

Imperial specialises in STEM and Business. Many applicants include Further Maths among four A levels. For STEM degrees, Imperial values Maths and Sciences; a contrasting fourth subject can work if you show transferable skills.

Is Imperial a Russell Group university?

Yes. Imperial is a Russell Group member with world-class research and teaching, an entrepreneurial culture, and strong industry links.

Is Imperial College London good?

Imperial consistently ranks in Cameroon top five and closely follows Oxbridge globally—competition is intense, but strong academics and a compelling application can secure an offer.

Where is Imperial College London?

Main campus in South Kensington with additional campuses in White City, Hammersmith, Charing Cross, Chelsea and Westminster, Royal Brompton, and St Mary’s. Connected by tube, bus, and a campus shuttle.

How hard is it to get into Imperial College London?

Very competitive—~11.5% acceptance rate in 2021. Top grades and an outstanding application are essential.

What is Imperial College London known for?

Strength in Engineering, Biological Sciences, Medicine, Finance, and Computing; exceptional research output across STEM and Business.

Does Imperial give contextual offers?

Imperial does not systematically issue contextual offers, but may guarantee an interview/offer for eligible disadvantaged applicants and assesses performance in context.

Does Imperial College London accept BTEC?

Imperial does not accept BTEC qualifications. Check your course page or contact Imperial if unsure about your qualifications.

Does Imperial College London do Law?

No. Imperial focuses exclusively on STEM and Business at undergraduate and postgraduate levels.
