1. Connect with LBS Early

One of the most effective and overlooked ways to strengthen your LBS MBA application is to connect with the school early. LBS actively encourages applicants to reach out before submitting their applications, reflecting its collaborative and relationship-driven culture.

Engage with LBS admissions staff

LBS admissions officers travel globally, hosting coffee chats, open days, and information sessions. These informal events allow you to:

  • Understand what LBS truly values in its candidates.
  • Ask insightful questions about the curriculum or leadership opportunities.
  • Build name recognition with admissions officers.

Connect with alumni and ambassadors

Networking with LBS alumni or current students through LinkedIn and school events demonstrates initiative. These interactions offer real-world insights into:

  • Student life and academic culture.
  • Clubs, electives, and leadership programmes.
  • How to express authentic motivation in essays and interviews.

There’s even a section in the LBS application to note who you’ve spoken to — use it to show initiative and engagement.

2. Plan Your GMAT Strategy Early

The GMAT or GRE is a critical component of your LBS MBA application. Many candidates underestimate the preparation needed and end up applying late or underperforming.

  • The average LBS GMAT score is around 700–720.
  • Start preparation 6–12 months before your target round.
  • Take timed practice tests to gauge progress.

If applying in later rounds, contact admissions about submitting your GMAT after initial review—flexibility may be possible for strong profiles.

3. Define Your Post-MBA Identity and Goals

LBS wants applicants with a clear post-MBA vision. Your goals should connect where you are now, why you need the MBA, and where you aim to go next.

Weak: “I want to start my own company.”

Strong: “After three years as a financial analyst, I aim to transition into strategy consulting, focusing on financial transformation.”

Align your ambitions with LBS’s track record for post-MBA employment to show mutual benefit and awareness.

4. Showcase the Qualities LBS Values Most

LBS assesses eight key criteria, focusing heavily on leadership, teamwork, and multiculturalism.

Leadership

Leadership isn’t just about title—it’s about initiative and influence. Highlight examples such as:

  • Owning a project or driving innovation.
  • Mentoring peers or leading a society.
  • Taking decisive action under uncertainty.

Connect this to LBS’s Leadership Institute and Global Experience Programme.

Teamwork

Show how you collaborate effectively in group settings:

  • Mediating conflicts or diverse opinions.
  • Contributing to cross-functional success.

Multiculturalism

LBS has students from over 60 nationalities. Showcase cultural awareness and adaptability through:

  • Work or study abroad experiences.
  • International projects or global volunteering.

5. Prepare Strategically for the LBS MBA Interview

LBS conducts two interviews: a pre-recorded Kira Talent assessment and a live interview with an admissions officer.

The Kira Talent Interview

This pre-recorded online assessment includes two 90-second questions. One is known in advance and one is surprise-based. Focus on:

  • Why LBS and why now?
  • Your leadership stories (use STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result).
  • Your professional and personal growth goals.

The Live Interview

Typically conducted by LBS staff, this session tests depth of motivation and cultural fit. Prepare by:

  • Researching your interviewer via LinkedIn.
  • Reviewing your essays thoroughly.
  • Practising two or three stories highlighting your leadership and teamwork.

FAQ: How to get into the London Business School MBA

What are the admission requirements for the London Business School MBA?
  • A recognised undergraduate degree or equivalent qualification.
  • A competitive GMAT or GRE score (average ~700).
  • 2–5 years of work experience with leadership and international exposure.
  • Proof of English proficiency (IELTS 7.0+ or TOEFL 100+).
  • Strong references and a career-focused personal essay.
What is the application process?
  1. Submit online application with transcripts, GMAT/GRE, and essays.
  2. Include details of work experience and achievements.
  3. Attend an MBA interview (Kira + live interview).
  4. Receive a final decision post-review.

Tip: Apply early in Round 1 or 2 for the best chance of acceptance.

What kind of candidates does LBS seek?

LBS values well-rounded professionals with academic excellence, leadership, and an international mindset. Ideal applicants demonstrate teamwork, cultural awareness, and impact across industries.

What is the structure of the LBS MBA programme?
  • 21-month flexible duration.
  • Core curriculum in finance, leadership, and strategy.
  • 80+ electives in entrepreneurship, innovation, and global business.
  • Summer internship and optional global experience.
How important are GMAT and GRE scores?

While important, GMAT/GRE is only one part of the application. Strong leadership and professional success can offset slightly lower scores.

What scholarships and funding options are available?
  • Merit-based scholarships for academic and leadership excellence.
  • Regional awards and need-based funding.
  • Loan programmes and company sponsorships.
How can international students apply?
  • Follow the standard MBA process.
  • Submit English proficiency proof if applicable.
  • Apply for a Cameroon visa upon acceptance.
  • Use LBS’s support for relocation and integration.
How can I improve my chances of acceptance?
  • Research and align your goals with LBS’s mission.
  • Demonstrate international and leadership experience.
  • Write genuine, detailed essays.
  • Prepare thoroughly for interviews.
  • Engage with LBS alumni to refine your insights.
