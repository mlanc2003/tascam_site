Parent Guide to Hiring a Tutor

Updated January 2026 · TASCAM Learning Support Team

Choosing the right tutor is one of the most important decisions a parent can make for their child’s education. This guide helps you understand what to look for, how to evaluate a tutor, and how to ensure your child gets the most out of every session.

1. What Makes a Good Tutor?

  • Subject expertise backed by real qualifications.
  • Patience and adaptability to meet individual learning needs.
  • Clear communication with both students and parents.
  • Demonstrated teaching experience in the relevant level.

2. How to Assess a Tutor Before Hiring

Before committing, parents should:

  • Review the tutor’s qualifications and background.
  • Ask for a short consultation or introductory meeting.
  • Check for experience with exam boards relevant to your child.
  • Discuss goals, expectations, and learning challenges.

3. Setting Goals With Your Child

  • Set clear academic targets together.
  • Identify strengths, weaknesses, and learning preferences.
  • Encourage open communication between you and the tutor.

4. Warning Signs to Watch Out For

  • Lack of feedback or progress reporting.
  • Guaranteeing unrealistic grades.
  • Lack of structure or unclear lesson planning.
